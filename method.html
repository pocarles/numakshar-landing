The method

Swiss precision, Indian depth.

NumAkshar didn't invent numerology. We built a bridge between a Swiss tradition refined over three decades and the Indian professional's instinct that numbers carry meaning. This is the story of how the method came together.

The origin

It started in Switzerland.

For over 30 years, a practice in Switzerland has been studying the patterns hidden in birth dates and names. Not to predict the future, but to help people understand what is already happening in their lives.

Thousands of personal readings. Thousands of hours refining a methodology that balances rigour with sensitivity. A tradition built on a simple conviction: when a reading is accurate, it doesn't frighten you. It calms you. It doesn't push you toward action. It gives you space to think.

The collaboration.

NumAkshar was born from a question: what happens when you take a methodology built for depth, and shape it for a culture that has always understood that numbers carry weight?

India doesn't need to be taught that numbers matter. From muhurat timing and naming ceremonies to business name selection and compatibility checks, the Indian relationship with numbers is ancient, intuitive, and deeply practical.

But the Indian numerology market today is largely built around quick consultations: a phone call, a lucky number, a spelling change. Valuable, but often surface-level. The reading ends when the call does.

NumAkshar's method takes a different approach. Working with Evan Verdier's three decades of Swiss practice, we co-developed a structured reading format: a written portrait that covers 33 sections, from core personality and career alignment to timing cycles and name vibration analysis.

The goal is simple. A single document that gives you everything. No follow-up needed to understand it. No dependency on a practitioner's availability. A reading you can return to at every crossroads.

Why two traditions are stronger than one.

Most Indian numerologists work with the Chaldean or Pythagorean systems, both of which originated outside India. Numerology has always been a practice that travels across borders and absorbs the intelligence of each culture it touches.

NumAkshar embraces this. The Swiss tradition brings a specific kind of rigour: structured analysis, written clarity, and a refusal to deal in fear or vague promises. The Indian context brings something the Swiss tradition alone cannot provide: a culture where numbers are already woven into daily decisions, from choosing a wedding date to naming a child to launching a company.

When you combine Swiss structure with Indian intuition, you get a reading that is both deeper and more practical than either tradition could produce alone.
